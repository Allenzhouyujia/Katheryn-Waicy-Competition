Why ResNet Works: Skip Connections Solve Deep Learning Challenges
Traditional Deep Networks

❌ Vanishing Gradients: Gradients become extremely small as they backpropagate through many layers

❌ Degradation Problem: Deeper networks paradoxically perform worse than shallow ones

❌ Training Difficulty: Very deep networks (50+ layers) are hard to train and often fail to converge

❌ Optimization Challenge: Learning identity mappings is difficult

ResNet with Skip Connections

✅ Gradient Highway: Skip connections provide direct paths for gradients to flow backward

✅ Performance Gain: Deeper networks consistently outperform shallow ones (152+ layers work well)

✅ Easier Training: Networks converge faster and more reliably

✅ Identity Learning: Network can learn F(x) = 0 to preserve identity when needed

ResNet-50 Architecture Summary

Total Layers: 50 layers (48 convolutional + 1 pooling + 1 FC)

Residual Blocks: 16 blocks (3+4+6+3) organized in 4 stages

Skip Connections: Each residual block has an identity shortcut

Key Innovation: H(x) = F(x) + x where F(x) is the residual mapping

Variants: ResNet-18, ResNet-34, ResNet-50, ResNet-101, ResNet-152

Parameters: ~25.6 million (ResNet-50)

Achievement: Won ILSVRC 2015 with 3.57% top-5 error rate
